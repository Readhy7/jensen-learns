# jensen-learns

A public log of my transition from sales leadership into technical AI work — specifically AI safety and evaluations.

I spent six years in sales at TTI/Milwaukee Tool and currently work in enrollment at UC Davis Continuing & Professional Education. In July 2026 I committed to a six-month plan: learn to code properly (unassisted — no AI writing my code), build real evaluation projects, and work toward roles in AI research and applied AI.

## What's here

- **[progress-log.md](progress-log.md)** — weekly timed solo coding assessments, logged honestly, including the failures. This is the receipt that the skills are mine.
- **assessments/** — the problems and my solutions, written under exam conditions (no AI, docs only, timed).
- **projects/** — portfolio projects, starting with small model evaluations and building toward serious safety-relevant evals.

## Ground rules I hold myself to

- **Tier 0 (Solo):** assessments and drills — no AI at all, documentation and search only.
- **Tier 1 (Tutor):** I write every line; AI explains concepts and reviews my code *after* it runs.
- **Tier 2 (Pair):** AI assistance allowed only for scaffolding (environment setup, CI, packaging) — never core logic.

If you're a hiring manager or program reviewer reading this: the commit history and progress log are the point. Watch the trajectory.
